Playable engineering case study

The Maestro’s Secret

A compact Renaissance point-and-click adventure built to reward close observation—and to make its product, accessibility, and AI-safety decisions easy to inspect.

Play (6–8 min) Engineering notes

Compact by design

One complete mystery: mirrored writing, a randomized route, item puzzles, generative lute music, and optional curiosities.

Static-first

The full adventure, resume path, and authored hints work without accounts, cookies beyond local save data, or a live AI service.

Inspectable judgment

OpenSpec scenarios, tests, and an evidence matrix make intended behavior and validation visible beside the game.

How it is made

Engineering notes

  • Architecture
    Vanilla ES modules separate puzzle state, persistence, and guidance rules from the illustrated scene runtime.
  • AI safety
    The optional guide uses fixed hint tiers and a whitelisted state summary. It never accepts player text or decides game state.
  • Accessibility
    Keyboard operation, visible focus, a high-contrast preference, reduced-motion support, and announced feedback are first-class requirements.
  • Quality
    OpenSpec scenarios map to test and manual evidence. Invalid saves and unavailable guidance fail safely to a playable baseline.

Intentional limits: no accounts, analytics dashboard, free-form chatbot, or model-dependent progression. The technical interest is in a small system with clear boundaries, not feature volume.
